Why Sleep Matters for Mental Health

  • Emotional Balance: Regulates brain chemicals like serotonin and dopamine, preventing mood swings.
  • Memory & Learning: Strengthens problem-solving and creativity.
  • Stress Reduction: Lowers cortisol (stress hormone).
  • Focus & Decisions: Improves concentration and judgment.
  • Prevention: Poor sleep increases risk of anxiety and depression.

Nighttime Routine (Step-by-Step)

  1. Wind Down (1 hr before bed): Dim lights, stop screens, do calm activities like reading or journaling.
  2. Relax Your Mind: Practice deep breathing, gratitude journaling, or meditation.
  3. Prepare Your Body: Drink warm water/herbal tea, stretch lightly, avoid caffeine/heavy meals.
  4. Set Sleep Environment: Keep room cool, dark, and quiet. Use bed only for rest.
  5. Bedtime Ritual: Read calming material, repeat positive affirmations, and keep a fixed bedtime.
